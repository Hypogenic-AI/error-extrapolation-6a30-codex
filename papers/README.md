# Downloaded Papers

This directory contains PDFs gathered for the Error Extrapolation resource phase.

1. [BooookScore: A systematic exploration of book-length summarization in the era of LLMs](2310.00785_booookscore_book_length_summarization.pdf)
   - Authors: Yapei Chang, Kyle Lo, Tanya Goyal, Mohit Iyyer
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00785
   - Why relevant: Directly studies LLM summaries of book-length inputs, chunking workflows, and coherence error types.

2. [Stress Testing Factual Consistency Metrics for Long-Document Summarization](2511.07689_stress_testing_long_document_factuality_metrics.pdf)
   - Authors: Zain Muhammad Mujahid, Dustin Wright, Isabelle Augenstein
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07689
   - Why relevant: Tests factuality metrics under long-document conditions and meaning-preserving perturbations.

3. [LongDocFACTScore: Evaluating the Factuality of Long Document Abstractive Summarisation](2309.12455_longdocfactscore_long_document_factuality.pdf)
   - Authors: Jennifer A. Bishop, Qianqian Xie, Sophia Ananiadou
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.12455
   - Why relevant: Proposes a retrieval-based factuality framework for long documents and releases LongSciVerify.

4. [A Tale of Evaluating Factual Consistency: Case Study on Long Document Summarization Evaluation](2025_findings_acl_long_document_summarization_evaluation_case_study.pdf)
   - Authors: Yang Zhong, Diane Litman
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: https://aclanthology.org/2025.findings-acl.648/
   - Why relevant: Studies factual-consistency evaluators across long-document datasets and model generations.

5. [Efficient Attentions for Long Document Summarization](2104.02112_govreport_efficient_attentions_long_summarization.pdf)
   - Authors: Luyang Huang, Shuyang Cao, Nikolaus Parulian, Heng Ji, Lu Wang
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.02112
   - Why relevant: Introduces GovReport, a large long-document summarization dataset.

6. [QMSum: A New Benchmark for Query-based Multi-domain Meeting Summarization](2104.05938_qmsum_query_based_meeting_summarization.pdf)
   - Authors: Ming Zhong et al.
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.05938
   - Why relevant: Provides query-based meeting summaries with long transcripts and relevant-span annotations.

7. [SQuALITY: Building a Long-Document Summarization Dataset the Hard Way](2205.11465_squality_long_document_summarization_dataset.pdf)
   - Authors: Alex Wang, Richard Yuanzhe Pang, Angelica Chen, Jason Phang, Samuel R. Bowman
   - Year: 2022
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11465
   - Why relevant: Provides high-quality long-input, question-focused summaries over short stories.

8. [SCROLLS: Standardized CompaRison Over Long Language Sequences](2201.03533_scrolls_long_sequence_benchmark.pdf)
   - Authors: Uri Shaham et al.
   - Year: 2022
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.03533
   - Why relevant: Standardizes long-text summarization, QA, and NLI tasks, including GovReport, SummScreenFD, and QMSum.

9. [ZeroSCROLLS: A Zero-Shot Benchmark for Long Text Understanding](2305.14196_zeroscrolls_long_text_benchmark.pdf)
   - Authors: Uri Shaham, Maor Ivgi, Avia Efrat, Jonathan Berant, Omer Levy
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14196
   - Why relevant: Evaluates LLMs zero-shot on long-text tasks, including summarization and aggregation.

10. [LongBench: A Bilingual, Multitask Benchmark for Long Context Understanding](2308.14508_longbench_long_context_benchmark.pdf)
    - Authors: Yushi Bai et al.
    - Year: 2024
    - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.14508
    - Why relevant: Provides long-context evaluation datasets and length-controlled variants.

11. [LongBench v2: Towards Deeper Understanding and Reasoning on Realistic Long-context Multitasks](2412.15204_longbench_v2_long_context_reasoning.pdf)
    - Authors: Yushi Bai et al.
    - Year: 2024
    - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.15204
    - Why relevant: Extends long-context evaluation to harder and much longer realistic inputs.

12. [Understanding Factuality in Abstractive Summarization with FRANK](2104.13346_frank_factuality_error_typology.pdf)
    - Authors: Artidoro Pagnoni, Vidhisha Balachandran, Yulia Tsvetkov
    - Year: 2021
    - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.13346
    - Why relevant: Provides fine-grained factual error categories and human annotations.

13. [Understanding Factual Errors in Summarization: Errors, Summarizers, Datasets, Error Detectors](2023_acl_aggrefact_understanding_factual_errors.pdf)
    - Authors: Liyan Tang et al.
    - Year: 2023
    - Source: https://aclanthology.org/2023.acl-long.650/
    - Why relevant: Aggregates factuality datasets and shows metrics vary across model eras and error types.

14. [SummaC: Re-Visiting NLI-based Models for Inconsistency Detection in Summarization](2111.09525_summac_inconsistency_detection.pdf)
    - Authors: Philippe Laban, Tobias Schnabel, Paul N. Bennett, Marti A. Hearst
    - Year: 2022
    - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.09525
    - Why relevant: Provides a practical NLI-based factual consistency baseline and benchmark.

15. [Evaluating the Factual Consistency of Abstractive Text Summarization](1910.12840_factcc_factual_consistency.pdf)
    - Authors: Wojciech Kryscinski, Bryan McCann, Caiming Xiong, Richard Socher
    - Year: 2020
    - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.12840
    - Why relevant: Introduces FactCC, a widely used factual consistency baseline.

16. [Evaluating the Factual Consistency of Large Language Models Through News Summarization](2023_findings_acl_fib_llm_factual_consistency.pdf)
    - Authors: Derek Tam, Anisha Mascarenhas, Shiyue Zhang, Sarah Kwan, Mohit Bansal, Colin Raffel
    - Year: 2023
    - Source: https://aclanthology.org/2023.findings-acl.322/
    - Why relevant: Introduces FIB for evaluating factual consistency preferences in LLMs.

17. [MiniCheck: Efficient Fact-Checking of LLMs on Grounding Documents](2404.10774_minicheck_llm_aggrefact.pdf)
    - Authors: Liyan Tang, Philippe Laban, Greg Durrett
    - Year: 2024
    - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.10774
    - Why relevant: Provides a strong, efficient sentence-level fact-checking baseline for grounded generations.

18. [Summarization is Not Dead Yet](2606.08000_summarization_is_not_dead_yet.pdf)
    - Authors: Dongqi Liu et al.
    - Year: 2026
    - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08000
    - Why relevant: Recent LLM-era reassessment of summarization quality, human references, and factuality.
